## SCRBrain: From Shared Neural Priors to Individual Perception via Biomimetic Dual-Stream Visual Reconstruction

The paper is currently under revision. The full code will be made publicly available after the paper is accepted.

# abstract
Reconstructing visual stimuli from functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) data helps elucidate human visual perception mechanisms. However, existing research is constrained by the scarcity of paired fMRI-image data, leading to an under-constrained high-dimensional neural decoding space and difficulty in establishing stable neural-visual mappings. Furthermore, most methods often prioritize semantic consistency while inadequately constraining low-level structural features. To this end, we propose SCRBrain, a biomimetic neural decoding framework inspired by the dual-stream mechanism of human vision. Adopting a progressive strategy of from shared neural priors to individual perception, SCRBrain aims to achieve both semantically faithful and structurally accurate visual reconstruction under limited fMRI-image data conditions. The framework comprises two complementary information flows. The high-level perception guidance flow integrates universal neural representations with subject-specific responses to generate semantic and geometric priors containing subjective intent. The visual reconstruction information flow employs an asymmetric dual-stream architecture to construct structured visual latent representations, establishing precise spatial layout and color initialization. These multi-level perceptual and structural priors are integrated to drive the neural decoding process, achieving high-fidelity visual reconstruction of complex visual stimuli. Experimental results show that SCRBrain outperforms state-of-the-art models across multiple quantitative and qualitative evaluation metrics. Moreover, our findings exhibit feature representations highly consistent with the hierarchical processing nature of human vision, further demonstrating the validity of the commonality extraction–individuality injection paradigm as a verifiable computational framework for neural decoding.
